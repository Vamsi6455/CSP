Conclusion & Inference

1. Awareness of Online Scams

Observation: Over 80% of respondents are aware of online scams.

Conclusion: Indicates growing awareness, possibly due to increased digital literacy and social media campaigns.

2. Scam Victim Percentage

Observation: Approximately 40% of participants have been scammed online at least once.

Conclusion: Despite awareness, many are still vulnerable to scams.

3. Types of Scams Faced

Scam TypeFrequency
Fake product deliveryHigh
Non-deliveryMedium
Payment fraudMedium
Phishing linksLow

Observation: Fake product delivery is the most common scam.

Conclusion: Stronger regulations and verification mechanisms are needed.

4. Actions Taken After Scam

Action TakenNumber of People
No action takenMajority
Reported to websiteModerate
Reported to policeFew

Observation: Most victims took no action due to lack of knowledge or confidence.

Conclusion: Need for better public guidance on scam reporting.

5. Trust in Online Shopping Post-Scam

Observation: Around 65% of victims trust online shopping less post-scam.

Conclusion: Scams negatively impact consumer confidence in e-commerce platforms.

6. Preferred Payment Methods

Payment MethodPreference Level
Cash on Delivery (COD)High
UPIMedium
Debit/Credit CardLow

Conclusion: COD remains a safe and trusted option due to fraud concerns.

7. Awareness Campaign Reach

Source of AwarenessReach Level
Social mediaHigh
Friends/PeersMedium
Official sourcesLow

Conclusion: Peer-based and social media awareness are effective tools for spreading scam prevention information.
